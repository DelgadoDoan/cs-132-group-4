RQ1: Spending Patterns

Time-series and comparative trend analysis across education, food, housing, and healthcare (2000–2025).

General Trend Overview

The plot above illustrates the evolution of household spending patterns over time. The data reveal consistent increases in education and healthcare expenditures, suggesting a long-term shift toward human capital investment and essential household services.

Spending on food, housing, and health continues to rise steadily, reflecting both inflationary pressures and changing consumption priorities.

Category-Level Analysis

The chart above compares quarterly Household Final Consumption Expenditure (HFCE) across multiple spending categories in the Philippines from 2000 to 2025. Each colored line represents a specific sector such as education, health, housing, food, and transport.

Among all sectors, Food and non-alcoholic beverages stands out as the fastest-growing category, surpassing others by a wide margin. This pattern highlights a steady increase in household investment toward long-term productivity and skill development. Meanwhile, essentials such as food, housing, and healthcare exhibit moderate but stable growth, showing resilience even during economic disruptions.

The synchronized upward trends across most categories indicate broad-based economic expansion and improved household welfare over the study period. However, sharper fluctuations around 2020 suggest the potential impacts of large-scale crises such as the COVID-19 pandemic.
