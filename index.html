Household Spending on Education

and its Economic Contribution

in the Philippines

Overview

The importance of education towards national development is well-established. It prepares citizens to meet the demands of a rapidly changing world and contribute meaningfully to society. Furthermore, it serves as the gateway for individuals to attain a better quality of life. In the Philippines, where disparities in access to quality education persist, understanding household spending towards education provides valuable insights into broader socio-economic dynamics.

This website presents an analysis of household expenditures on education from 2000 to 2025 and explores how these spending patterns relate to the Gross Value Added (GVA) of the education sector in the Philippines. We make use of nationally sourced datasets and perform statistical methods such as time-series analysis to examine long-term trends and causal/interrupted-series methods to examine responses to national economic shocks such as the recent COVID-19 pandemic. The results reveal a compelling trend where household spending on education not only correlates strongly with national economic growth, but also demonstrates remarkable elasticity and recovery towards the said major crisis events.

Background

Education is an important asset in economic and social development. In the Philippines, households play a major role in financing education because of the large presence of private schools and training institutions. This makes household spending a key factor in determining access to education and its overall contribution to the economy. From 2000 to 2025, the Philippines experienced major economic changes, including growth periods, natural disasters, and the COVID-19 pandemic. These events affected how families spent on education and how the education sector contributed to the economy. This study examines whether household investments in education are linked to the sector’s Gross Value Added (GVA) and how spending patterns change during economic shocks.

Research topic

How has household spending on education changed from 2000 to 2025 and how has this influenced the economic contribution of the education sector in the Philippines?

Research questions

[RQ1] How have household expenditures on education, relative to other major household spending categories, changed from 2000 to 2025?
[RQ2] To what extent does household expenditure on education correlate with changes in the gross value added (GVA) in education?
[RQ3] How did household spending on education change during national economic crises (e.g. 2013 Typhoon Yolanda and 2020 COVID-19)?

Hypotheses

H0,1: Household expenditures on education have no significant effect on the GVA in education.
HA,1: Household expenditures on education have a significant effect on the GVA in education.

Our aim is to contribute insights on how education spending impacts national economic outcomes.

Data

We consolidated official datasets from OpenStat, the official data portal of the Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA) to build a reproducible foundation for our analyses.

Sample size: 1248

Gross Value Added (GVA) in Education

Methods

The following section outlines the methods and analytical approaches used to address each research question. Each page presents the relevant statistical technique, its purpose, and how it contributes to understanding household expenditure behavior in the Philippines. Our analysis pipeline combines statistical tests, visualization, and robust checks to ensure validity.

Nutshell: Education & GVA Overview

This summary visualization presents the 25-year relationship between household education expenditure and Gross Value Added (GVA). It highlights synchronized growth trends, disruptions from key crises such as Typhoon Yolanda (2013) and COVID-19 (2020), and the rapid post-crisis recoveries that demonstrate education's resilience as a household priority.

Tools: Python (pandas, matplotlib, seaborn).

RQ1: Spending Patterns

This analysis employs time-series and comparative trend analysis to examine household spending across key categories such as education, food, housing, and healthcare from 2000 to 2025. The corresponding graphs visualize changes in consumption priorities over time and reveal long-term expenditure trends that reflect economic and social development.

Tools: Python (pandas, matplotlib, seaborn).

RQ2: Relationship with GVA

Correlation analysis using Pearson's method of correlation is applied to evaluate the relationship between household education expenditure and Gross Value Added (GVA) from 2000 to 2025. The plotted graphs display how these two indicators move over time and help determine the strength and direction of their association. Log transformations are performed to normalize distributions and improve the accuracy of statistical inference.

Tools: Python (numpy, scipy, matplotlib, seaborn).

RQ3: Crisis Impact

Interrupted Time Series (ITS) analysis is conducted to assess how major crises such as Typhoon Yolanda (2013) and the COVID-19 pandemic (2020) affected household spending on education. The graph visualizes points of disruption in the expenditure trend and helps quantify both short-term and long-term effects. This method provides a clear understanding of how external shocks influence household economic behavior.

Tools: Python (numpy, scipy, statsmodels, matplotlib, seaborn).
