RQ3: Crisis Impact

Interrupted time-series analysis to capture spending shifts during major crises such as Typhoon Yolanda (2013) and COVID-19 (2020).

Interpretation

The figure above displays the Household Final Consumption Expenditure (HFCE) in education from 2000 to 2025, modeled using an Interrupted Time Series (ITS) approach.

Noticeable breaks appear around 2013 and 2020 — corresponding to Typhoon Yolanda and the COVID-19 pandemic, respectively. After Yolanda, a short-term dip was followed by recovery and continued growth, whereas COVID-19 caused a more sustained disturbance in spending behavior before normalization resumed.

These interruptions highlight how large-scale crises can temporarily alter education spending trends, emphasizing the need for resilient household expenditure strategies and stronger government support mechanisms.
