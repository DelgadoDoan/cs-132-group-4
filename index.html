SDG 4: Quality Education

Household Spending on Education and its Economic Contribution in the Philippines (2000–2025)

This project investigates household expenditures on education from 2000 to 2025 and explores how these spending patterns relate to the Gross Value Added (GVA) of the education sector in the Philippines. We employ nationally-sourced datasets, time-series analysis, and causal/interrupted-series methods to examine long-term trends and responses to economic shocks.

Overview

Research topic, background, objectives, questions and hypotheses.

Background

Education is an important asset in economic and social development. In the Philippines, households play a major role in financing education because of the large presence of private schools and training institutions. This makes household spending a key factor in determining access to education and its overall contribution to the economy. From 2000 to 2025, the Philippines experienced major economic changes, including growth periods, natural disasters, and the COVID-19 pandemic. These events affected how families spent on education and how the education sector contributed to the economy. This study examines whether household investments in education are linked to the sector’s Gross Value Added (GVA) and how spending patterns change during economic shocks.

Research topic

How has household spending on education changed from 2000 to 2025 and how has this influenced the economic contribution of the education sector in the Philippines?

Research questions

[RQ1] How have household expenditures on education, relative to other major household spending categories, changed from 2000 to 2025?
[RQ2] To what extent does household expenditure on education correlate with changes in the gross value added (GVA) in education?
[RQ3] How did household spending on education change during national economic crises (e.g. 2013 Typhoon Yolanda and 2020 COVID-19)?

Hypotheses

H0,1: Household expenditures on education have no significant effect on the GVA in education.
HA,1: Household expenditures on education have a significant effect on the GVA in education.

Our aim is to contribute insights on how education spending impacts national economic outcomes.

Proposed methodology

Methods we will use to answer each research question.

RQ1: Spending patterns

Time-series and comparative trend analysis across education, food, housing, and healthcare (2000–2025). Tools: Python (pandas, numpy, matplotlib).

RQ2: Relationship with GVA

Correlation tests (Pearson) to explore association between household education spending and GVA. Tools: Python (scipy, statsmodels).

RQ3: Crisis impact

Interrupted time series analysis to capture spending shifts during 2013 (Typhoon Yolanda) and 2020 (COVID-19). Tools: Python (statsmodels).

Our analysis pipeline combines statistical tests, visualization, and robust checks to ensure validity.
